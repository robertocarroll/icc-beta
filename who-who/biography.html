Judge Sang-Hyun Song

President of the International Criminal Court

Appeals Division
Republic of Korea - Elected from the Asian Group of States, list A

Overview

Judge Sang became a judge on 11 March 2003 for a term of three years and was re-elected in 2006 for a further full term of nine years.

He has been President of the Court since 2009. He was elected for a term of three years on 11 March 2009 and re-elected on 11 March 2012 for another three years.

Biography

Judge Song (1941) has extensive practical and academic experience in the areas of court management, civil and criminal procedure, and the law of evidence. For more than thirty years, he taught as a professor of law at Seoul National University Law School. He has held visiting professorships at law schools, including Harvard, New York University, Melbourne and Wellington.

Judge Song started his legal career as a judge-advocate in the Korean army and later as a foreign attorney in a New York law firm.

He has served as a member of the advisory committee to the Korean Supreme Court and the Ministry of Justice. He led initiatives to reform the national litigation system and the criminal justice system, particularly the reform of the Penal Code, the Code of Criminal Procedure, the Court Rules of Criminal Procedure, and the prison system.

Judge Song is co-founder of the Legal Aid Centre for Women, and of the Childhood Leukaemia Foundation in Seoul, and the President of UNICEF/KOREA. Judge Song is also the respected author of several publications on relevant legal issues, and the recipient of the highest decoration of the Korean Government (MUNGUNGHWA, 2011).
